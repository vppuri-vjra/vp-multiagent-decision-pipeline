# DECISION MEMO
**Customer/Revenue Lens**

## RECOMMENDATION
**Recommend Option A (Launch Q3 pilot with top 2 SKUs) contingent on confirming baseline metrics within 1 week and establishing clear kill switch thresholds.** This balances speed-to-market for competitive positioning with controlled risk exposure, while maximizing learning velocity to capture early adopter revenue and validate subscription appetite before competitors move.

## OPTIONS_CONSIDERED

**Option A: Launch pilot in Q3 with top 2 SKUs**
- Fastest path to market capture and revenue generation
- Limits complexity and operational burden for pilot phase
- Enables rapid customer feedback on core value proposition
- Risk: May select wrong SKUs without full analysis; potential value perception gap if offering too narrow

**Option B: Delay until full product catalog analysis is complete**
- Maximizes confidence in SKU selection and value proposition design
- Reduces risk of value perception failure with comprehensive offering
- Risk: Misses Q3 market timing; competitors may launch first; analysis paralysis delays revenue opportunity; erodes stakeholder confidence in execution speed

**Option C: Launch limited free-tier first to gauge interest**
- Lowest customer acquisition friction; builds adoption baseline
- Tests operational readiness without revenue pressure
- Risk: Severely limits CLV impact (free ≠ revenue); creates difficult paid conversion path; may attract wrong customer segment; trains customers to expect free benefits; unclear path to $213 CLV increase ($1,420 × 15%)

## TRADEOFFS

**Gain vs Risk Analysis:**

- **GAIN:** First-mover advantage captures high-value customers before market saturation; premium positioning strengthens brand perception as market leader  
  **RISK:** Rushed execution with incomplete SKU analysis damages brand with early adopters who are most vocal advocates/detractors

- **GAIN:** 5% pilot limits downside revenue exposure while generating statistically meaningful signal on $71M+ revenue base (assumes 50K+ customers at $1,420 CLV)  
  **RISK:** May cannibalize existing full-price purchases from highest-value 5% segment, reducing overall revenue even if subscription adoption succeeds

- **GAIN:** Subscription model creates predictable recurring revenue stream and increases purchase frequency, potentially achieving 15% CLV lift through behavior change  
  **RISK:** Exclusive discounts erode margin on purchases that would have occurred at full price; priority shipping adds variable cost without offsetting revenue

- **GAIN:** Early access and exclusivity benefits deepen customer engagement and emotional loyalty beyond transactional relationship  
  **RISK:** Non-subscribers (95% of base) perceive tiering as penalty, reducing satisfaction and increasing churn among excluded majority

## RISKS_AND_MITIGATIONS

**Revenue & Financial Risks:**
- **Risk:** Margin erosion if discount burn rate + priority shipping costs exceed incremental revenue lift  
  *Mitigation:* Establish minimum 20% contribution margin threshold as kill switch criterion; model discount caps (e.g., 10% max) and shipping cost per order before launch

- **Risk:** Cannibalization of high-margin purchases by existing top-tier customers  
  *Mitigation:* Track control group (non-pilot customers matched by purchase history) to measure incremental vs. substitution impact; require minimum 10% net revenue increase to proceed

- **Risk:** Insufficient revenue base to absorb subscription infrastructure investment (billing platform, fulfillment modifications, support staffing)  
  *Mitigation:* **MUST CONFIRM:** Current revenue run rate and cost structure can support estimated $[X] implementation cost within existing budget

**Customer Experience Risks:**
- **Risk:** Value perception failure if benefits don't justify subscription cost in customer eyes  
  *Mitigation:* **NEED TO MEASURE:** Survey pilot cohort willingness-to-pay and perceived value vs. price within first 2 weeks; minimum 60% "good value" threshold

- **Risk:** Operational failure (late shipments, billing errors) with pilot cohort damages relationships with best customers  
  *Mitigation:* Require operations sign-off on priority shipping SLA (e.g., 95% on-time) before launch; white-glove support for pilot cohort

- **Risk:** Non-subscriber alienation creates net satisfaction decline across customer base  
  *Mitigation:* Frame pilot as "exclusive early access" with broader rollout planned; maintain baseline product experience quality for all customers

**Execution Risks:**
- **Risk:** Two-week decision timeline forces choice without critical SKU performance, margin, and segmentation data  
  *Mitigation:* Implement phased go-decision: Week 1 = data gathering + provisional green light; Week 2 = final commit with kill switch defined

- **Risk:** Kill switch unavailable or stakeholders unable to pull trigger on failing pilot due to sunk cost fallacy  
  *Mitigation:* Define hard thresholds NOW (e.g., <3% conversion after 4 weeks, >25% churn after 8 weeks, NPS decline >10 points) with auto-pause built into system
